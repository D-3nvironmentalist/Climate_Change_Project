C02 Levels and Global Temperature Anamolies

Carbon dioxide (CO2) is an important heat-trapping (greenhouse) gas, which is released through human activities such as deforestation and burning fossil fuels, as well as natural processes such as respiration and volcanic eruptions. Ever since after the industrial revoluton there has been a steady increase in Carbon Dioxide in the atmosphere which is the driving force behing climate change. One of the most obvious signals of climate change is the rise in global average temperature over the past several decades. Comparing the average temperature of land, ocean, or land and ocean combined for any month or multi-month period to the average temperature for the same period over the 20th century shows if conditions are warmer or cooler than the past. This graph shows the steady increase of Carbon Dioxide levels in Parts Per Million (ppm) starting from 1958 up until April of 2020.
